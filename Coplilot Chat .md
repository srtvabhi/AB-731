## 1. Strategic Decision Framing – Market Expansion

**Complete Prompt**

Act as a senior strategy advisor to the CEO.

Our company is a mid-sized B2B software provider with $120M annual revenue, growing at 6% YoY, primarily in North America.

We are considering entering the Southeast Asian market within the next 12 months. Current data:
- Customer acquisition cost in our core market: $8,500  
- Average annual contract value: $42,000  
- Gross margin: 68%  
- No local partners in Southeast Asia  
- Two global competitors already operate there with localized offerings  

Perform the following:
1. Identify the key strategic assumptions behind this expansion  
2. Highlight financial, operational, and brand risks if assumptions fail  
3. Propose three strategic paths (conservative, balanced, aggressive)  
4. Recommend what evidence or signals leadership should gather before committing  

**Leadership Skill:** Strategic judgment under uncertainty

## 2. Executive-Level Problem Diagnosis – Revenue Stagnation

**Complete Prompt**

Act as a senior business diagnostician reporting to the executive committee.

Business context:
- Revenue growth has slowed from 12% to 4% over 18 months  
- Customer churn increased from 6% to 11%  
- Sales headcount grew by 25%, but pipeline conversion dropped  
- Product release cycles slipped from quarterly to biannual  

Diagnose the situation using first-principles thinking:
1. Separate symptoms vs root causes  
2. Categorize root causes across people, process, technology, and market  
3. Identify which issues require leadership action vs operational fixes  
4. Recommend the single highest-leverage executive intervention  

**Leadership Skill:** Organizational problem-solving

## 3. Competitive Intelligence Synthesis – Industry Disruption

Competitor data:

1. Competitive positioning summary (who wins where and why)  
2. Each competitor’s strategic strengths and vulnerabilities  
3. Likely moves in the next 6–12 months  
4. Defensive and offensive strategies for a market leader to protect share

## 4. Board-Level Narrative Creation – Strategic Reset

**Complete Prompt**

Act as a chief strategy officer preparing a board narrative.

Context:
- Customer expectations are shifting toward integrated, AI-enabled solutions  
- Our current product portfolio is modular and requires heavy customization  
- Operating costs have risen 14% while pricing power is declining  
- New entrants are winning deals by positioning as “future-ready platforms”  

Create a board-ready narrative that:
1. Explains what has fundamentally changed in the market  
2. Clarifies why the current strategy is no longer sufficient  
3. Articulates a clear strategic shift  
4. Frames the risk of inaction in business terms  

Keep the tone executive, concise, and non-technical.

## 5. Scenario Planning with External Shock – Regulatory & AI Impact

**Complete Prompt**

Run a structured scenario-planning exercise for the executive team.

Assumptions:
- A new regulation will require greater transparency in AI-driven decision systems within 18 months  
- Compliance costs are estimated at 3–5% of annual operating expense  
- Customers increasingly demand AI-enabled features  

Create three scenarios:
- Best Case: Regulation favors large incumbents  
- Base Case: Moderate compliance burden, uneven enforcement  
- Worst Case: High compliance cost, delayed product innovation  

For each scenario, provide:
1. Expected business impact  
2. Strategic risks  
3. Executive actions required in the first 90 days  

**Leadership Skill:** Risk management & foresight

## 6. Leadership Communication Stress Test – Change Announcement

**Complete Prompt**

Act as an executive communication advisor.

Context:
- The company is restructuring to reduce operating costs by 10%  
- No layoffs in the next 6 months, but roles and reporting lines will change  
- Employee engagement scores dropped from 78% to 66% in the last year  

Draft leadership message:
> “To remain competitive, we are adjusting our operating model.  
> These changes are necessary for long-term success and will improve efficiency across teams.”

Perform the following:
1. Identify ambiguities, trust risks, and emotional triggers in the message  
2. Predict likely employee reactions (high performers, middle managers, frontline staff)  
3. Rewrite the message to improve clarity, confidence, and credibility  
4. Suggest two follow-up leadership actions to reinforce trust  

**Leadership Skill:** Change communication & credibility
